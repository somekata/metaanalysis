Guide to RoB 2.0: Risk of Bias Tool for RCTs

The RoB 2.0 tool assesses the risk of bias in randomized controlled trials (RCTs) based on five specific domains. A judgment (Low, Some Concerns, High) is made for each domain, leading to an overall judgment.

Source: Cochrane RoB 2.0 Website

The Five Domains of RoB 2.0

Domain 1: Bias arising from the randomization process

Question: Is the assignment to intervention groups random and concealed?

Focus: Was the sequence generation truly random, and was allocation concealment adequate?

Domain 2: Bias due to deviations from intended interventions

Question: Were participants and personnel blinded, and was the intervention administered as intended?

Focus: Were there differences in care received between groups other than the intervention itself? Was blinding successful?

Domain 3: Bias due to missing outcome data

Question: Is the outcome data complete for all randomized participants?

Focus: Was the amount and nature of missing data similar across groups? Was the missing data appropriately addressed (e.g., intention-to-treat analysis)?

Domain 4: Bias in measurement of the outcome

Question: Was the outcome assessment biased due to knowledge of the intervention received?

Focus: Was the outcome measured in the same way for all groups? Were the outcome assessors blinded?

Domain 5: Bias in selection of the reported result

Question: Was the reported outcome selected based on the results?

Focus: Did the study report pre-specified outcomes and analyses, or were there selective omissions based on statistical significance?

Overall Risk of Bias Judgments

The overall judgment for the study is derived from the judgments across the five domains:

  • Low Risk of Bias: Low risk for all five domains.
  • Some Concerns: Some concerns in at least one domain, but not high risk.
  • High Risk of Bias: High risk in at least one domain, or some concerns in multiple domains that raise serious doubts.
