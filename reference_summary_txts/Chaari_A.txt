2. Population
Target condition / disease definition: Candidemia defined as isolation of Candida species from ≥1 set of blood cultures collected ≥48 h after ICU admission (Page 2, "Definitions")
Sample size (total): 51 (Table 1; Abstract)
Sample size (per group):

C. auris group: 27 (Table 1; Abstract)

Non-C. auris group: 24 (Table 1; Abstract)
Age: Median 66 [56–78] years overall; 63 [52–73] vs 67 [62.3–83] (CA vs NCA) (Table 1)
Sex: Gender (M/F) overall 24/27; CA 11/16; NCA 13/11 (Table 1)
Inclusion criteria: "All adult patients with candidemia admitted between 01.01.2022 and 31.03.2024" (Abstract); adult patients aged ≥18 years with candidemia screened for inclusion (Page 2, "Patients and data collection")
Exclusion criteria: SARS-CoV-2 infection; moribund; life expectancy <1 month due to underlying malignancy (Page 2, "Patients and data collection")
Handling of multiple episodes: Only first candidemia episode included; recurrence defined as new episode ≥1 month after resolution (Page 2, "Patients and data collection")

3. Exposure / Intervention / Comparison
Exposure / Intervention: Candidozyma auris candidemia (Abstract; Page 2–3 "Two groups were compared…")
Comparison group: Non-Candidozyma auris candidemia (Abstract; Page 2–3 "Two groups were compared…")
Definition of groups (e.g., auris vs non-auris): Species identification/confirmation using MALDI-TOF MS; "all isolated were confirmed using the Bruker Biotyper… updated FDA-approved… library (Versions 2019)" (Page 2, "Microbiology")
Follow-up period: 28-day mortality assessed "following candidemia onset" (Page 2, end of "Patients and data collection")

4. Outcomes
Primary outcome (with timepoint): Not reported (no explicit primary outcome stated)
Secondary outcomes: Duration of mechanical ventilation; ICU length of stay; ICU mortality; 28-day mortality following candidemia onset (Page 2, end of "Patients and data collection")
Outcome definitions:

28-day mortality: "28-day mortality following candidemia onset" (Page 2, end of "Patients and data collection")

Septic shock definition: Sepsis-3 criteria (Page 2, "Definitions")
Measurement methods: Electronic medical record review (Page 2, "Patients and data collection")

5. Results
A) Association of C. auris vs non-C. auris with 28-day mortality
Effect size (RR / OR / HR): OR = 0.3 (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
95% Confidence Interval: 95% CI [0.1–1.1] (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
p-value: p = 0.08 (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
Adjusted variables (list all): SAPSII (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
Adjusted or unadjusted: Both reported

Unadjusted: OR = 0.3, 95% CI [0.1–1.1], p = 0.08 (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")

Adjusted for SAPSII: OR = 0.3, 95% CI [0.1–1.2], p = 0.087 (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
Model type (e.g., Cox regression, logistic regression): Not reported for mortality comparison (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")

B) Predictors of C. auris candidemia (risk factor analysis)
Effect size (RR / OR / HR): Prior exposure to antifungals: OR = 6.5 (Table 3)
95% Confidence Interval: 95% CI [1.7–25.3] (Table 3)
p-value: p = 0.007 (Table 3)
Adjusted variables (list all): Variables included in multivariate model: duration of invasive venous lines; previous exposure to antifungals; previous exposure to ceftazidime-avibactam; previous exposure to tigecycline; colonization with CRE prior to candidemia (Table 3)
Adjusted or unadjusted: Adjusted (multivariate) (Table 3; Page 2, "Statistical analysis")
Model type (e.g., Cox regression, logistic regression): "Binary stepwise regression" (Page 2, "Statistical analysis")

7. Risk of Bias Related Information (NOS domains)
Selection
Representativeness of cohort: ICU patients with candidemia from two tertiary hospitals; all adult candidemia cases during study period screened (Abstract; Page 2 "Study design"; Page 2 "Patients and data collection")
Exposure ascertainment method: Species confirmation by MALDI-TOF MS using updated FDA-approved library; PCR used for screening colonization after implementation (Page 2, "Microbiology")

Comparability
Confounding adjustment (variables and method):

Predictors of C. auris candidemia adjusted via "binary stepwise regression" including variables listed in Table 3 (Page 2 "Statistical analysis"; Table 3)

Mortality comparison additionally adjusted for SAPSII (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
Outcome
Outcome assessment method: Clinical outcomes assessed via electronic medical record review; mortality endpoints include ICU mortality and 28-day mortality (Page 2, "Patients and data collection")
Adequacy of follow-up: 28-day mortality assessed after candidemia onset (Page 2, end of "Patients and data collection"); completeness not reported

8. Confounding (Important)
Key confounders considered: SAPSII; SOFA; Charlson Comorbidities Index recorded (Table 1; Page 2 "Patients and data collection"); SAPSII used for adjustment in mortality comparison (Page 5, "Outcome and prognostic factors")
Residual confounding discussed: Small sample size limits robustness of multivariate analysis (Page 6, "limitations… small sample size (n=51) remains a limiting factor for robust multivariate analysis")
Methods used (matching / multivariable / propensity score): Multivariable "binary stepwise regression" (Page 2 "Statistical analysis"); no matching/propensity score reported
