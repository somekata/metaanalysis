2. Population
Target condition / disease definition: Candidemia defined operationally as "at least one blood culture… that grew Candida species" (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Sample size (total): 201 patients (Table 1; Page 2, "Results—Appearance of C. auris")
Sample size (per group):

C. auris: 77 (Table 1)

C. albicans: 50 (Table 1)

Other Candida spp.: 74 (Table 1)
Age: Mean age years (SD): C. auris 58 (20); C. albicans 48 (30); other 56 (25) (Table 1)
Sex: Male: C. auris 43 (56%); C. albicans 26 (52%); other 44 (60%) (Table 1)
Inclusion criteria: Patients treated at AKUHN with "at least one blood culture between September 2010 and December 2016 that grew Candida species" (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Exclusion criteria: Not reported

3. Exposure / Intervention / Comparison
Exposure / Intervention: Candida auris candidemia (Title; Abstract)
Comparison group: C. albicans candidemia and "other Candida species" candidemia (Abstract; Table 1)
Definition of groups (e.g., auris vs non-auris): Vitek2 identified isolates as Candida haemulonii; "species determinations for 21 of the isolates using published molecular and proteomic methods identified all as C. auris." (Abstract) and "Twenty-one of the isolates identified by Vitek2 as Candida haemulonii were subjected to… ITS sequencing… PFGE… MALDI-TOF… All 21 were identified as C. auris." (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Follow-up period: In-hospital mortality reported (Table 1). Additional early mortality assessment within "a week" after initial blood culture described (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information"; Page 4, "Outcome of patients with candidemia"). Exact follow-up duration for primary mortality analysis beyond "in hospital" not reported.

4. Outcomes
Primary outcome (with timepoint): Not reported
Secondary outcomes: Not reported (explicitly labeled)
Outcome definitions:

Death in hospital: Reported as "Death in hospital" (Table 1)

Attribution review (mortality contribution): Review for "clinical deterioration or death within the period of two days before to a week after the death and absence of a more likely cause" (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Measurement methods:

Identification of candidemia cases via "electronic medical record (EMR)" and "written records were reviewed" (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")

Blood culture system: BD BACTEC (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")

Species identification: VITEK 2 Compact; subset confirmation by ITS sequencing/PFGE/MALDI-TOF (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")

Effect size (RR / OR / HR):

Carbapenem (Days): OR 1.19 (1.05–1.35) (Table 3)

Model type (e.g., Cox regression, logistic regression): "forward selection in a logistic regression model" (Table 3)

7. Risk of Bias Related Information (NOS domains)
Selection
Representativeness of cohort: Single-facility referral center cohort; included patients with at least one Candida-positive blood culture during Sept 2010–Dec 2016 (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Exposure ascertainment method:

Candidemia ascertained via EMR identification and review (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")

Species identification primarily by Vitek2; subset confirmation for 21 presumed C. haemulonii isolates via ITS sequencing/PFGE/MALDI-TOF (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information"; Abstract)

Comparability
Confounding adjustment (variables and method): Logistic regression with forward selection; variables stated as age, critical care unit, presence of CVC (Table 3). (Note: mortality not adjusted.)

Outcome
Outcome assessment method: In-hospital death (Table 1); mortality attribution assessed by medical record review (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Adequacy of follow-up: In-hospital mortality captured (Table 1); completeness of follow-up not otherwise reported.

8. Confounding (Important)
Key confounders considered: Age, critical care unit status, presence of CVC (Table 3)
Residual confounding discussed: Not reported
Methods used (matching / multivariable / propensity score): Multivariable logistic regression with forward selection (Table 3); no matching/propensity score reported

9. GRADE-related Supporting Information
Study design: Retrospective observational analysis (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
Risk of bias (supporting details):

Reliance on EMR and written record review (Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")

Identification limitation: Vitek2 initially identified isolates as C. haemulonii; only 21 isolates underwent molecular/proteomic confirmation as C. auris (Abstract; Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")

Susceptibility testing limitation explicitly noted: "Vitek2 system has not been shown to be reliable for testing C. auris" (Page 3–4, "In vitro susceptibilities…")
Inconsistency (if discussed): Not reported
Indirectness (population/intervention differences): Not reported
Imprecision (CI width, event numbers): Not reported (mortality effect estimate with CI not provided; only crude event counts)
Publication bias (if mentioned): Not reported

10. Final Assessment
Suitable for meta-analysis: Yes (Table 1 provides extractable in-hospital mortality event counts by species group)
Reason if not suitable: Not applicable
Notes for inclusion (e.g., outcome definition differences, population issues):

Mortality endpoint is in-hospital, not 28- or 30-day (Table 1)

C. auris identification was confirmed by advanced methods for 21 isolates originally called C. haemulonii by Vitek2; applicability of this confirmation to all C. auris cases in Table 1 is not explicitly stated (Abstract; Page 2, "Methods—Patient information")
