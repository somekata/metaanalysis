2. Population
Target condition / disease definition: Candidemia defined as "at least one blood culture positive for Candida species within a 14-day window" (Page 2, "Methods—Definitions")
Sample size (total): 320 patients (Table 1; Page 3, "Results")
Sample size (per group):

C. auris: 110 (Table 1)
C. albicans: 120 (Table 1)
Other Candida spp.: 90 (Table 1)
Age: Mean age years (SD): C. auris 62 (18); C. albicans 55 (22); other 59 (20) (Table 1)
Sex: Male: C. auris 65 (59%); C. albicans 70 (58%); other 52 (58%) (Table 1)
Inclusion criteria: Adults (>=18 years) admitted between January 2015 and December 2020 with at least one Candida-positive blood culture (Page 2, "Methods—Inclusion criteria")
Exclusion criteria: Patients with polymicrobial candidemia or incomplete medical records (Page 2, "Methods—Exclusion criteria")

3. Exposure / Intervention / Comparison
Exposure / Intervention: Candida auris candidemia (Title; Abstract)
Comparison group: C. albicans candidemia and other Candida species candidemia (Abstract; Table 1)
Definition of groups: Species identified by MALDI-TOF MS; C. auris confirmed by ITS sequencing for all isolates initially flagged as C. haemulonii or C. duobushaemulonii (Page 2, "Methods—Microbiological identification")
Follow-up period: 30-day all-cause mortality (Table 1; Page 3, "Results—Primary outcome")

4. Outcomes
Primary outcome (with timepoint): 30-day all-cause mortality (Table 1)
Secondary outcomes: ICU length of stay; duration of antifungal therapy (Page 3, "Results—Secondary outcomes")
Outcome definitions:

30-day mortality: Death from any cause within 30 days of index blood culture (Page 2, "Methods—Definitions")
Attribution review (mortality contribution): Adjudicated by two independent investigators; discordance resolved by a third reviewer (Page 2, "Methods—Outcome assessment")
Measurement methods:

Candidemia cases identified via hospital microbiology database and confirmed by chart review (Page 2, "Methods")
Blood culture system: BacT/ALERT (bioMerieux) (Page 2, "Methods")
Species identification: MALDI-TOF MS (Bruker); ITS sequencing for confirmation of C. auris (Page 2, "Methods")

5. Results
A) 30-day mortality (crude; by species)
Effect size (RR / OR / HR): Not reported (unadjusted counts only)
95% Confidence Interval: Not reported
p-value: C. auris vs C. albicans p=0.032; C. auris vs other p=0.210 (Table 1)
Adjusted variables (list all): Not reported
Adjusted or unadjusted: Unadjusted (Table 1)
Model type (e.g., Cox regression, logistic regression): Not reported

B) Multivariate analysis of 30-day mortality
Effect size (RR / OR / HR):

C. auris (vs C. albicans): OR 2.34 (1.18-4.65) (Table 3)
Prior antifungal exposure: OR 1.87 (1.10-3.18) (Table 3)
ICU admission: OR 3.05 (1.54-6.04) (Table 3)
Central venous catheter: OR 2.41 (1.25-4.65) (Table 3)
p-value: 0.015; 0.021; 0.001; 0.009 respectively (Table 3)
Adjusted variables (list all): Age, sex, ICU admission, central venous catheter, prior antifungal exposure, Charlson Comorbidity Index (Table 3)
Adjusted or unadjusted: Adjusted (multivariate logistic regression) (Table 3)
Model type (e.g., Cox regression, logistic regression): Multivariate logistic regression (Table 3)

7. Risk of Bias Related Information (NOS domains)
Selection
Representativeness of cohort: Multicenter academic hospitals; consecutive eligible patients included; may not represent community settings (Page 2, "Methods—Study setting")
Exposure ascertainment method:

Candidemia confirmed by positive blood culture with species-level identification (Page 2, "Methods")
C. auris confirmed by ITS sequencing for all initially ambiguous isolates (Page 2, "Methods")

Comparability
Confounding adjustment (variables and method): Multivariate logistic regression adjusting for age, sex, ICU status, CVC, antifungal exposure, Charlson Comorbidity Index (Table 3)

Outcome
Outcome assessment method: 30-day all-cause mortality adjudicated by independent reviewers (Page 2, "Methods—Outcome assessment")
Adequacy of follow-up: 97.5% follow-up completeness; 8 patients excluded due to loss to follow-up (Page 3, "Results")

8. Confounding (Important)
Key confounders considered: Age, sex, ICU admission, CVC presence, prior antifungal exposure, Charlson Comorbidity Index (Table 3)
Residual confounding discussed: Authors note potential residual confounding by unmeasured severity of illness markers (Page 5, "Discussion—Limitations")
Methods used (matching / multivariable / propensity score): Multivariable logistic regression; no matching or propensity score reported (Table 3)

9. GRADE-related Supporting Information
Study design: Retrospective multicenter cohort study (Page 2, "Methods")
Risk of bias (supporting details):

Retrospective design with reliance on medical record review (Page 2, "Methods")
Species confirmation limited to isolates flagged by MALDI-TOF as ambiguous; all C. auris isolates confirmed by ITS sequencing (Page 2, "Methods")
Antifungal susceptibility testing performed by broth microdilution per CLSI M27-Ed4 (Page 3, "Results—In vitro susceptibilities")
Inconsistency (if discussed): Not reported
Indirectness (population/intervention differences): Academic medical centers only; may limit generalizability to community hospitals (Page 5, "Discussion")
Imprecision (CI width, event numbers): Adjusted OR for C. auris mortality 2.34 (1.18-4.65); wide CI reflecting moderate sample size (Table 3)
Publication bias (if mentioned): Not reported

10. Final Assessment
Suitable for meta-analysis: Yes (Table 1 provides extractable 30-day mortality event counts by species group)
Reason if not suitable: Not applicable
Notes for inclusion (e.g., outcome definition differences, population issues):

Mortality endpoint is 30-day, not in-hospital; may differ from studies using in-hospital mortality
C. auris confirmed by ITS sequencing for all isolates; high confidence in species identification
Multicenter design increases generalizability compared to single-center studies
