Flowchart of Systematic Review and Meta-analysis

  1. Conceptualize and Scope the Review Idea

    Identify a broad topic of interest and conduct preliminary (informal) searches to gauge the existing literature, feasibility, and potential relevance of a systematic review.

    This phase determines if a review is necessary or if sufficient primary data exists.

  2. Define the research question (PICO)

    Clearly specify the research question based on Population, Intervention, Comparison, and Outcome.

    The foundation of the entire review. Ambiguity here leads to unreliable results.

  3. Set databases and search keywords

    Determine which databases to use (e.g., PubMed, Cochrane, Scopus) and develop search strategies.

    Document the search terms and logic beforehand.

  4. Establish inclusion and exclusion criteria

    Define which types of studies will be included or excluded.

    e.g., Only RCTs, adult patients, English-language studies.

  5. Develop and register the study protocol

    Prepare a detailed methodology and register it (e.g., in PROSPERO).

    Prevents selective reporting and bias.

  6. Conduct literature search

    Perform the database searches according to the defined strategy.

    Export and organize all search results.

  7. Assess the quality of included studies

    Evaluate risk of bias using standardized tools (e.g., RoB 2, Newcastle–Ottawa).

    Consider excluding low-quality studies.

  8. Extract and collect data

    Gather relevant data from all eligible studies.

    Double-check data extraction for accuracy.

  9. Perform meta-analysis

    Conduct statistical pooling and create forest plots and other visual summaries.

    Quantitatively synthesize evidence.
