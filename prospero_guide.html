Guide to PROSPERO: Systematic Review Protocol Registration

PROSPERO is an international database of prospectively registered systematic reviews. Registering your protocol helps prevent unnecessary duplication and promotes transparency in reporting.

Website: https://www.crd.york.ac.uk/prospero/

1. Before You Start: Prerequisites

Crucial Step: Your review must be registered **before** you complete the selection of studies for inclusion (i.e., before data extraction begins).

  • You need a detailed **Protocol** document ready (including PICO, search strategy, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and data synthesis methods).
  • You must create a free user account on the PROSPERO website.

2. Registration Process Overview

The registration process involves completing a detailed online form. Key sections include:

  1. Review Title and Authors: Provide the review title and all research team members.
  2. Research Question (PICO): Clearly state the primary and secondary research questions.
  3. Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria: Specify the eligibility criteria for study selection.
  4. Search Strategy: Upload or describe the complete search strategy used across all databases.
  5. Data Extraction/Synthesis: Detail how data will be extracted, and the statistical methods (e.g., meta-analysis) planned.

3. Submission and Confirmation

After completing all mandatory fields, submit the form.

  • The submission will be reviewed by the PROSPERO team for completeness.
  • Upon acceptance, your review will be assigned a unique **PROSPERO Registration Number (e.g., CRD42025XXXXX)**, which should be included in your final publication.
